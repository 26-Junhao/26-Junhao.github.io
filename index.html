DARTS

Origin

The origins of darts can be traced back to medieval times in England.
Soldiers during the Middle Ages used to throw short arrows at the bottom of a wine barrel or a wooden target to pass the time.
As the game evolved, the wooden target became a circular board, and the arrows were eventually replaced with darts.

Evolution

The game's popularity increased in the 19th century, particularly in the United Kingdom.
It was played as a pub game, with various rules and regional variations. The standardization of the game
began in the early 20th century when the National Darts Association (NDA) was formed in Britain.

Standardization

The NDA, established in 1924, played a significant role in standardizing the rules and regulations of darts.
They introduced the numbering system we see on dartboards today, where the numbers 1 to 20 are arranged in a specific pattern.
The bullseye, the central circle, was also included as a high-scoring area.

Television Era

Darts gained popularity in the 1970s, thanks in part to the advent of televised tournaments.
The first significant televised darts competition was the News of the World Championship, which began in 1970.
The game's exposure on TV helped it reach a broader audience and established several legendary players as household names.

Professionalization

With increased media coverage and growing interest, the game became more professional.
The British Darts Organisation (BDO) was established in 1973 to govern and promote the sport.
However, in 1993, several top players broke away from the BDO to form the World Darts Council
(now known as the Professional Darts Corporation or PDC), which focused on more lucrative tournaments and higher prize money.

Popularity Boom

The 2000s saw a significant boom in darts' popularity, largely due to the PDC's efforts,
new formats, and charismatic players like Phil Taylor, Michael van Gerwen, and Gary Anderson.
The PDC's World Championship, held annually, became one of the most prestigious and highly watched events in the sport.

Global Reach

Darts is no longer limited to the United Kingdom, as it has gained a substantial global following.
Countries such as the Netherlands, Germany, and Australia have produced top-class players and host popular tournaments.
Today, darts continues to be a beloved sport with a dedicated fan base, regular tournaments,
and professional players competing at the highest level. It remains a staple in pubs and bars around the world,
providing entertainment and friendly competition for people of all ages.
