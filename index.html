D A R T S

Origin

The origins of darts can be traced back to medieval times in England.
Soldiers during the Middle Ages used to throw short arrows at the bottom of a wine barrel or a wooden
target to pass the time. As the game evolved, the wooden target became a circular board, and the arrows were eventually replaced with darts.

Evolution

The game's popularity increased in the 19th century, particularly in the United Kingdom.
It was played as a pub game, with various rules and regional variations. The standardization of the game
began in the early 20th century when the National Darts Association (NDA) was formed in Britain.

Standardization

The NDA, established in 1924, played a significant role in standardizing the rules and regulations of darts.
They introduced the numbering system we see on dartboards today, where the numbers 1 to 20 are arranged in
a specific pattern. The bullseye, the central circle, was also included as a high-scoring area.

Television Era

Darts gained popularity in the 1970s, thanks in part to the advent of televised tournaments.
The first significant televised darts competition was the News of the World Championship, which began in 1970.
The game's exposure on TV helped it reach a broader audience and established several legendary players as household names.

Professionalization

With increased media coverage and growing interest, the game became more professional.
The British Darts Organisation (BDO) was established in 1973 to govern and promote the sport.
However, in 1993, several top players broke away from the BDO to form the World Darts Council
(now known as the Professional Darts Corporation or PDC), which focused on more lucrative tournaments and higher prize money.

Popularity Boom

The 2000s saw a significant boom in darts' popularity, largely due to the PDC's efforts,
new formats, and charismatic players like Phil Taylor, Michael van Gerwen, and Gary Anderson.
The PDC's World Championship, held annually, became one of the most prestigious and highly watched events in the sport.

Global Reach

Darts is no longer limited to the United Kingdom, as it has gained a substantial global following.
Countries such as the Netherlands, Germany, and Australia have produced top-class players and host popular tournaments.
Today, darts continues to be a beloved sport with a dedicated fan base, regular tournaments,
and professional players competing at the highest level. It remains a staple in pubs and bars around the world,
providing entertainment and friendly competition for people of all ages.

Straight Barrel

A straight barrel is a cylindrical-shaped dart with a consistent diameter from front
to back. It offers a simple and straightforward design, suitable for players who
prefer a consistent grip and release.

Front-Weighted Barrel

As mentioned earlier, a front-weighted barrel has more weight towards the front end.

Phil Taylor

Phil Taylor is a legendary professional darts player from England, widely regarded as one of the
greatest players in the history of the sport. He was born on August 13, 1960, in Stoke-on-Trent, Staffordshire, England.
Phil Taylor is often referred to by his nickname "The Power" due to his dominant performances and incredible success throughout his career.
He has won numerous darts tournaments and championships, including a record 16 World Championships as of September 2021.

Taylor's dominance in the sport has earned him admiration from darts enthusiasts and players alike.
He has been instrumental in popularizing darts and played a significant role in raising the profile of the game globally.

Paul Lim

Paul Lim is a professional darts player from Singapore, known for achieving one of the most memorable
moments in darts history. He was born on January 25, 1954, in Singapore. Paul Lim is widely recognized for hitting the
first televised nine-dart finish in the history of the World Darts Championship. He achieved this remarkable feat during
the 1990 Embassy World Darts Championship,

which took place at the Lakeside Country Club. This achievement earned him the nickname "The Singapore Slinger"
and made him a popular figure in the darts world.

Michael van Gerwen

Michael van Gerwen, often referred to as "MvG," is a Dutch professional darts player and one of the most successful
and popular figures in the history of the sport. He was born on April 25, 1989, in Boxtel, Netherlands.
Van Gerwen made his mark in the darts world at an early age and quickly rose to prominence.
He won his first major title in 2006 by claiming the World Masters Youth Championship. Since then,
he has had a remarkable career and has become one of the dominant forces in professional darts.

Michael van Gerwen is known for his incredible scoring ability, precision on the doubles, and his intense competitiveness.
He has a very aggressive and high-scoring style of play, earning him the nickname "Mighty Mike."
